# Training Impact of Teams

## **Importance of Cyber Security Training**

- Cyber security expertise is built through **continuous practice and learning**, not innate ability.
- Training requires **safe, isolated environments** (e.g., labs) to avoid harming production systems.
- Training serves as **preventive preparation**, improving readiness and reducing real-world incident risks.
- Well-trained teams handle incidents more efficiently, **boosting capacity without expanding headcount**.
- Enables **junior staff to ramp up faster**, reducing the mentoring burden on senior members.
- Establishes a **centralised baseline** for measuring and tracking skill development.
- Encourages **team building and engagement**, especially through interactive exercises like **Capture the Flag (CTF)** events.

## **Training in Large Organisations**

- Small teams often use **off-the-shelf training** solutions.
- Large or specialised teams (>20 people) benefit from **customised training** tailored to internal needs.
- **TryHackMe’s Content Studio** allows creation or modification of training modules aligned with company priorities.
- Large enterprises seek **integration with existing IT ecosystems**, requiring:
    - **SSO (Single Sign-On)** for streamlined access
    - **APIs** for interoperability with internal tools

## **Making a Business Case for Training**

- **Investment rationale**: Employee training = organisational asset growth.
- **Example calculation**:
    - Team: 10 employees
    - Annual cost per employee: **$80,000**
    - Productivity increase from training: **4%**
    - Total productivity gain: **$32,000** (10 × 4% × $80,000)
    - Training cost: **$5,000** (10 × $500)
    - **ROI: 640%** ($32,000 ÷ $5,000)
- Conclusion: Training offers **substantial financial and operational returns**.

## **Vendor Selection Criteria**

Key questions when evaluating training vendors:

- **Target audience**: Who is the training for (roles, experience, goals)?
- **Vendor relevance**: Do they have experience with similar organisations?
- **Content quality**: Does the training offer depth, breadth, and relevance?
- **Platform capability**: Can users **learn, practice, and assess skills** in one environment?
- **Integration & scalability**: Support for **SSO and APIs**.
- **Cost vs. value**: Training expenses are minor compared to gains in **team efficiency and risk reduction**.

## **Key Takeaway**

Cyber security training is a **high-ROI investment** that enhances skills, team performance, and organisational resilience. Through structured, customised, and integrated programs, companies can create a culture of continuous learning while maximising productivity and preparedness.
