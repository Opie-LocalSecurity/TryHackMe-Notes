# OWASP Top 10 2025: IAAA Failures

## **IAAA Framework**

A model for verifying users and their actions. Each step depends on the previous—no skipping allowed.

- **Identity** — Unique identifier for a user/service (user ID, email).
- **Authentication** — Proves the identity (password, OTP, passkeys).
- **Authorisation** — Defines what an authenticated identity can do.
- **Accountability** — Logs and alerts showing who did what, when, and from where.

Weaknesses in IAAA lead directly to several OWASP Top 10 risks, including A01, A07, and A09.

## **A01: Broken Access Control**

Failure to consistently enforce *who* can access *what* on **every request**.

### Key Concepts

- **IDOR (Insecure Direct Object Reference):** Manipulating identifiers (e.g., `?id=7` → `?id=6`) to access another user’s resources.
- **Horizontal privilege escalation:** Accessing another normal user’s data.
- **Vertical privilege escalation:** Accessing admin-only actions.

### Outcome

Attackers can view or modify data that does not belong to them.

## **A07: Authentication Failures**

Occurs when an application cannot reliably verify or bind a user’s identity.

### Common Issues

- Username enumeration
- Weak passwords or no rate limiting
- Logic flaws in login/registration
- Insecure session or cookie handling

### Example Scenario

- Registering `aDmiN` to impersonate `admin` due to poor username uniqueness enforcement.

### Outcome

Attackers can log in as other users or hijack sessions.

## **A09: Logging & Alerting Failures**

Missing or inadequate logs prevent detection, analysis, and response to attacks.

### Symptoms

- Missing authentication/authorization events
- Unclear error messages
- No alerts for suspicious patterns (e.g., brute force)
- Short log retention or tamperable log storage

### Role

Enables **Accountability**—tracking actions and proving what occurred during an incident.

### Outcome

Investigations become difficult or impossible; attackers can operate without detection.
