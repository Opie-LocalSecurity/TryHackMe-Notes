# The CIA Triad

The **CIA Triad** is the foundational model for cybersecurity. It defines the three core objectives required to protect digital data and systems. Security is not merely the presence of tools, but the successful maintenance of these three conditions.

## 1. Confidentiality (Privacy)

**Core Concept:** Ensuring that sensitive information is accessible **only** to authorized individuals or systems.

- **Failure Outcome:** Unauthorized access, data leaks, privacy violations, and legal/financial penalties.
- **Digital Tools:** * **Encryption:** Scrambling data so it is unreadable without a key.
    - **Access Controls:** Permissions and authentication (e.g., passwords, MFA) to restrict entry.
- **Practical Example:** Preventing a "Man-in-the-Middle" attack on public Wi-Fi where credentials are intercepted.

## 2. Integrity (Trustworthiness)

**Core Concept:** Ensuring that data remains accurate and has not been **modified** by unauthorized parties.

- **Failure Outcome:** Data becomes untrustworthy; unauthorized changes (e.g., altering a bank recipient or a student's grade) lead to incorrect or dangerous outcomes.
- **Methodology:** Verification processes and digital signatures to ensure data at rest and in transit remains in its original state.
- **Practical Example:** A bank transaction must arrive at the destination with the exact amount and account number specified by the sender.

## 3. Availability (Uptime)

**Core Concept:** Ensuring that systems, networks, and data are **accessible** to authorized users when needed.

- **Failure Outcome:** Business downtime, loss of revenue, and inability to access critical services (e.g., a bank being closed during a power failure).
- **Threats:** Denial of Service (DoS) attacks, where attackers flood a system with traffic to crash it.
- **Methodology:** * **Redundancy:** Using backup power or servers.
    - **Load Balancing:** Managing traffic thresholds to prevent system overloads.

## The Cybersecurity Mindset

The CIA Triad serves as a **diagnostic framework** for security professionals. When an incident occurs, it is evaluated by asking:

1. **Confidentiality:** Was data exposed?
2. **Integrity:** Was data altered?
3. **Availability:** Was service disrupted?

| **Pillar** | **Focus** | **Key Threat** |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Confidentiality** | Who can see it? | Unauthorized disclosure / Leaks |
| **Integrity** | Is it correct? | Unauthorized modification / Tampering |
| **Availability** | Can I use it? | Denial of Service / System outages |
