# Computer Types

### 1. General-Purpose Computing

These systems are designed for direct human interaction and vary based on the balance between **portability** and **performance**.

- **Laptop:** Prioritizes mobility and battery life. Form factor constraints lead to thermal management challenges, which can result in performance throttling during sustained heavy workloads.
- **Desktop:** Designed for a fixed location using AC power. Superior cooling systems allow for higher sustained performance and consistency compared to laptops.
- **Workstation:** A high-end desktop variant optimized for **professional accuracy and reliability**. They utilize specialized hardware (e.g., ECC memory) to prevent errors during long-duration simulations or complex 3D rendering.

### 2. Infrastructure and Specialized Systems

These systems often operate without a dedicated user interface (headless) and focus on specific operational roles.

- **Server:** High-availability machines designed to provide services to multiple users over a network. They are built for continuous operation and high throughput.
- **Smartphone/Tablet:** Highly integrated, touch-first mobile devices optimized for connectivity and energy efficiency.
- **Embedded Computer:** A single-purpose computer integrated into a larger mechanical or electrical system (e.g., a coffee maker or door sensor). They often perform "invisible" logic for the life of the device.
- **IoT (Internet of Things) Device:** A subset of embedded systems characterized by **network connectivity**.

### 3. Key Distinctions & Methodologies

| **Feature** | **Embedded Computer** | **IoT Device** |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Primary Goal** | Executing a specific task within a device. | Data reporting or remote command execution. |
| **Connectivity** | Often offline; localized control. | Network-dependent (Wi-Fi, Zigbee, LTE). |
| **User Interaction** | Minimal to none. | Remote via apps or dashboards. |

> **The Performance Rule:** As the physical size of a device decreases (from Desktop to Laptop to Smartphone), thermal management becomes the primary bottleneck for sustained computational power.
