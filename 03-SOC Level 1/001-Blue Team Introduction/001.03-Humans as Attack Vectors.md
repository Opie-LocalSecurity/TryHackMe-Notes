# Humans as Attack Vectors

## 🔐 **The Human Element in Cybersecurity**

### **Core Concept**

- Humans are often the weakest link in cybersecurity — attackers exploit *human psychology* rather than technical vulnerabilities.
- Social engineering tactics trick victims into *voluntarily giving access* (e.g., through phishing or impersonation).

## 🎯 **Why Humans Are Targeted**

- Attackers pursue humans for the *access* they control: accounts, systems, and data.
- Access can enable:
    - Theft of sensitive data (e.g., HR databases)
    - Financial fraud (e.g., hijacking banking sessions)
    - Network infiltration (e.g., admin VPN accounts)
    - Espionage or lateral attack facilitation (e.g., government secrets)

## 🧠 **Social Engineering Attacks**

Rely on **trust** and **emotion** to manipulate victims.

### 1. **Phishing**

- Fake, urgent-looking messages (usually emails) lure victims to malicious links or attachments.
- Goal: steal credentials or deliver malware.
- ~3.4 billion phishing emails are sent daily.

### 2. **Malware Downloads**

- Users are tricked into installing malware disguised as legitimate software.
- Common lures: fake CAPTCHAs, QR codes, or SEO-poisoned websites.

### 3. **Deepfakes**

- AI-generated audio/video impersonations used to deceive victims (e.g., fake executive calls).
- Example: A finance worker was conned into wiring $25M using a deepfake video call.

### 4. **Impersonation (Non-AI)**

- Attackers pose as trusted figures (IT staff, management) via phone, email, or message.
- Common entry point for ransomware groups.

### 5. **Other Methods**

- USB drop campaigns, insider threats, fake job offers, and physical intrusions.

## 🛡️ **Defending Humans**

### **Two Main Goals**

1. **Mitigation** – Prevent or reduce the chance of successful attacks.
2. **Detection** – Identify and investigate attacks that bypass defenses.

### **Key Mitigation Measures**

| Measure | Purpose |
| --- | --- |
| **Anti-phishing solutions** | Block phishing emails before reaching users. |
| **Antivirus / EDR** | Prevent malware execution on endpoints. |
| **“Trust but verify” principle** | Train staff to validate suspicious requests or media (e.g., deepfakes). |
| **Security awareness training** | Educate employees via simulations and continuous training. |

## **SOC Analyst Role**

- Detect and analyze attacks that evade mitigation.
- Recommend and implement improved defenses to reduce SOC workload and organizational risk.

## **Takeaway**

> Cybersecurity is not only about firewalls and encryption — it’s about people.
> 
> 
> Attackers exploit human trust; defenders must combine technical tools and human awareness to stay ahead.
