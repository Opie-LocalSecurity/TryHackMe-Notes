# Junior Security Analyst Intro

## **Role Focus**

- Entry-level cybersecurity position, typically called **Tier 1 SOC Analyst** or **Triage Specialist**.
- Main responsibility: **Monitor, triage, and escalate alerts** from various security tools and event logs in a 24x7 Security Operations Center (SOC).

## **Core Responsibilities**

- **Monitor & Investigate Alerts:** Review event logs and security alerts to identify potential threats.
- **Configure Security Tools:** Assist in managing and updating systems like firewalls, IDS/IPS, and SIEM tools.
- **Develop IDS Signatures:** Create simple intrusion detection rules.
- **Ticketing & Escalation:** Open, document, and escalate incidents to Tier 2 or Team Lead.
- **Collaboration:** Participate in SOC meetings and operational updates.

## **Required Skills & Qualifications**

- **Experience:** 0–2 years in security operations or IT.
- **Knowledge Areas:**
    - Networking (OSI/TCP-IP models)
    - Operating Systems (Windows, Linux)
    - Web applications
- **Scripting/Programming:** Basic automation skills are beneficial.
- **Certifications:** *CompTIA Security+* is widely recommended.

## **SOC (Security Operations Center) Structure**

**Purpose:** Centralized team that continuously **monitors, detects, investigates, and responds** to cybersecurity threats.

**Tiers Overview:**

- **Tier 1 (Junior Analyst):** Alert monitoring and triage.
- **Tier 2:** In-depth incident analysis and remediation.
- **Tier 3:** Advanced threat hunting and forensic investigations.

## **SOC Key Responsibilities**

### **1. Preparation & Prevention**

- Stay updated on **cyber threat intelligence (CTI)** through platforms like *Twitter* and *Feedly*.
- Maintain and harden systems via:
    - Firewall and IDS/IPS updates
    - Vulnerability patching
    - Blacklisting/safelisting apps, IPs, and emails
- Understand **TTPs (Tactics, Techniques, and Procedures)** of adversaries (e.g., *CISA APT40 reports*).
- Develop and follow a **security roadmap** for proactive defense.

### **2. Monitoring & Investigation**

- Use tools like **SIEM (Security Information and Event Management)** and **EDR (Endpoint Detection and Response)** to track anomalies.
- Prioritize alerts by severity: *Critical → High → Medium → Low*.
- Conduct **triage analysis** to determine the nature and scope of incidents.
- Correlate logs, use open-source intelligence, and answer key investigative questions (“How, When, Why?”).

### **3. Response**

- Coordinate containment and remediation steps:
    - Isolate infected hosts
    - Terminate malicious processes
    - Remove or quarantine malicious files

## **A Day in the Life**

- **Start of Shift:** Review new alerts and tickets in the queue.
- **Routine Tasks:**
    - Analyze IDS/IPS alerts and suspicious emails
    - Examine forensic artifacts
    - Use OSINT (open-source intelligence) for validation
- **Incident Handling:** Perform detection, containment, and remediation as needed.
- **Outcome:** Successful mitigation of threats — one of the most rewarding parts of the role.

## **Career Progression**

- **Tier 1 (Junior Analyst)** → **Tier 2 (Incident Responder)** → **Tier 3 (Threat Hunter / Forensics Expert)**
- Growth comes from mastering alert triage, investigation, scripting, and analytical techniques.

## **In essence:**

A Junior Security Analyst is the SOC’s first line of defense — continuously monitoring for potential threats, performing triage, escalating incidents, and contributing to the organization’s overall cybersecurity resilience.
