# SOC L1 Alert Reporting

## 🧭 **Alert Funnel Overview**

- **Alert Flow:**
    1. **L1 Analysts** receive alerts via SIEM, EDR, or ticket systems.
        - Most alerts are closed as *False Positives*.
        - Complex or severe ones are *escalated to L2*.
    2. **L2 Analysts** handle deeper investigation and remediation.
    3. **DFIR Team** engages only for major incidents.
- **Core Processes After Triage:**
    - **Reporting** → document findings.
    - **Escalation** → pass complex alerts to L2.
    - **Communication** → coordinate with other departments (IT, HR, etc.).

## 📝 **Alert Reporting**

**Purpose of Reports:**

| Function | Explanation |
| --- | --- |
| **Context for Escalation** | Helps L2 understand what happened without redoing analysis. |
| **Record Keeping** | Alerts (with reports) are retained indefinitely, unlike SIEM logs. |
| **Skill Development** | Writing reports enhances analytical clarity and reasoning. |

**Report Format – *Five Ws* Approach:**

- **Who:** Involved user or account.
- **What:** Actions or events performed.
- **When:** Exact time range of activity.
- **Where:** Devices, IPs, or domains.
- **Why:** Analyst reasoning and verdict justification.

## 🚨 **Alert Escalation**

### **When to Escalate to L2:**

1. Indicators of a major attack requiring deeper investigation or DFIR.
2. Remediation actions needed (e.g., malware removal, host isolation).
3. Communication required with external parties or leadership.
4. L1 is uncertain about the alert or needs senior support.

### **Typical Escalation Steps:**

1. Finalize your alert report and verdict → mark alert “In Progress.”
2. Reassign to **L2 on shift** and notify them (chat or in person).
3. L2 reviews report, investigates further, and may trigger **Incident Response** if necessary.

## 🗣️ **SOC Communication**

**Common Scenarios & Responses:**

- **L2 unavailable:** Escalate via emergency contacts → L2 → L3 → Manager.
- **User account compromise:** Never contact through breached channels; use phone or email.
- **Alert surge:** Prioritize critical alerts, notify L2.
- **Missed malicious activity:** Inform L2 immediately — delayed response can be costly.
- **SIEM issues:** Investigate what you can; report parsing/search problems to L2 or SOC engineers.

**L2 Communication Responsibilities:**

- L2 analysts coordinate cross-department actions (Legal, PR, DFIR) for major incidents such as data breaches.

## ⚙️ **Key Takeaways**

- **L1 analysts** are the first defense layer: triage → report → escalate.
- **Effective reporting** ensures smooth handoff and preserves investigation context.
- **Proper escalation** avoids delays and ensures major threats reach the right team.
- **Clear communication** prevents errors, maintains security posture, and ensures accountability across teams.
