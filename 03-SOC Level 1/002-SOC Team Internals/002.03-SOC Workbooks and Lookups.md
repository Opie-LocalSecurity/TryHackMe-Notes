# SOC Workbooks and Lookups

## 🧩 **Assets & Identities Overview**

Used by SOC analysts to understand **who** performed an action, **where**, and **why**.

### **Scenario Example**

Alert: *G.Baker logged into HQ-FINFS-02 and shared a file with R.Lund.*

To triage, analysts must understand:

- **Who** G.Baker and R.Lund are (roles, permissions, normal behavior)
- **What** HQ-FINFS-02 is (purpose, access level)
- **Why** data transfer occurred (legitimate or suspicious?)

## 👤 **Identity Inventory**

A **catalogue of all user and service accounts**, including privileges, contact info, and roles.

Used to **add context** during incident analysis.

### **Example Entries**

| User | Role | Access | Location |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| G.Baker | CFO | VPN, HQ, FINANCE | UK |
| R.Lund | US Financial Adviser | VPN, FINANCE | US |
| svc-veeam-06 | Backup Service Account | VEEAM, DMZ, HQ | N/A |

## 🌐 **Network Diagrams**

Visual **maps of network topology**—locations, subnets, and interconnections.

Used to **interpret IPs and attack paths** in firewall/SIEM logs.

### **Use Case Example**

- External IP `103.61.240.174` brute-forces **VPN (TCP/10443)**
- Gains VPN access → assigned internal IP `10.10.0.53`
- Scans internal subnets (`172.16.15.0/24`, `172.16.23.0/24`)
- Firewall limits scanning; attacker shifts targets

**Purpose:** Helps analysts visualize attack progression, understand subnet roles (e.g., VPN, Office, Database), and correlate alerts.

## 📘 **SOC Workbooks (Playbooks / Runbooks)**

Structured **step-by-step guides** for analysts to triage specific alert types consistently.

- Prepared by **senior analysts**
- Followed by **L1 analysts** to ensure no investigation step is missed
- Provide **efficiency, accuracy, and repeatability**

### **Example: Unusual Login Location Workbook**

Divided into **three main stages**:

1. **Enrichment** – Gather context
    - Use Threat Intel and Identity Inventory to understand the user, IP, and system.
2. **Investigation** – Analyze data
    - Review SIEM logs and determine if login behavior is legitimate.
3. **Escalation** – Take action
    - Escalate to L2 or verify directly with the user if suspicious.

## **Key Takeaways**

- **Identity Inventory:** Who performed the action?
- **Asset Inventory:** Where did it occur?
- **Network Diagram:** How did it happen (network context)?
- **Workbooks:** What should the analyst do next?

Together, these tools form the foundation of **structured, evidence-based SOC investigations**.
